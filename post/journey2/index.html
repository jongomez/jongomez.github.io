Journey Week 2/26

Journey Week 2/26

Mar 24, 2017 #Journey

2 weeks have passed. This second week was also a bad one. However, it was way better than the first week. Mainly because I worked way harder on my wordpress stuff. Which was nice. This increase in productivity was mainly due to something really stupid I did: I created a “default new tab page” on my browser, with some pictures of my parents, some motivational quotes and images. So everytime I opened up a new tab, I’d get a message from my parents, saying “Don’t waste your life ya fucking dummy”. As stupid as this sounds, I think it worked.

I also came up with what I believe to be a really good idea for an online business involving wordpress. In fact, I thought it was such a great idea that I threw out lot of the stuff I did on week 1, just to work on this new idea.

Despite having done a lot of hard work, I constantly felt like I wasn’t working on the best things. For example, I spent around 1 hour trying to find a domain name. And after I found a good one, I kept going back trying to test other domains. Same thing with finding a wordpress theme for my site. But hey, at least now I’ve got a domain and a theme.

Also did a little bit of work on my thesis, but not much. Not much progress on my weightloss sub-journey either.

On this upcoming 3rd week, I hope to focus more on my thesis. I also hope to work not only harder, but smarter. How am I going to work smarter? Here are a few suggestions for myself:

  • Do a lot more planning. “Plans are useless, but planning is indispensable”, said Eisenhower (I think). Not sure if I agree with the “plans are useless” part, but I definitely agree that having a “planning” habit is more useful than just writing down plans on a piece of paper every once in a while.

  • Don’t let your mind wander (wonder?). Your basic/default state of mind is not a good one. This is a state of mind where any thought can take over your mind. Avoiding this state of mind is going to take effort, but ultimately will improve your life.

  • Take breaks and meditate for about 5 to 10 minutes. It’s not going to be easy, but it’s going to be worth it.

Anyway, I’ll probably forget about all this in the morning. SEE YA.
