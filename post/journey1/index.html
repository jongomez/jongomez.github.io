Journey Week 1/26

Journey Week 1/26

Mar 17, 2017 #Journey

This first week was not a good one. I only spent around 10 hours working on sales stuff. I could’ve done that in 1 day if I put my mind into it. These 10 hours were spent reading reading stuff, searching for niches, methods, and so on. I’m still going to focus on wordpress themes, but now I’ve decided not to start developing themes right away. I’ll just focus on selling existing themes, via affiliate programs. The envato market has an affiliate program I’d like to try out.

Also, I lost 2 kilos this week, which wasn’t bad. So I’m now at 102 kg.

Didn’t work on my master thesis.

Overall, I’m really disappointed with my progress. I lacked the discipline to work on the things that matter the most. The things that will make my life better. Here’s where I think I failed:

  • Not working when I’m feeling down or tired - This happens a lot. If I’m not feeling okay, it’s really hard for me to do whatever. Gotta learn how to brush small issues off, and work on what’s important, whether I feel like it or not.

  • Lack of focus - I can’t do anything for 10 minutes straight without going on reddit at least once. Gotta work on this.

Major lessons learned this week:

  • Thoughts are important. I should strive to always have the best possible thoughts and mental state. However, my mental state and thoughts tend to be average / bad. Very rarely do I find myself truly motivated. So I gotta be able to get things done, regardless of my mental state. This is SUPER hard. It’s one of those things I hear people say a lot… but I don’t think most people can pull this off. So I gotta pay very close attention to my thoughts and mental state, but don’t rely on them entirely to get things done. One way to accomplish this is by asking myself “Can I do X right now?”, and answer with a number ranging from 1 to 10. 1 means I absolutely cannot do X right now. 10 means I’m absolutely motivated to do task X right now. If the answer is between 1-3, it’s best to just take a short break.

  • Don’t just focus on doing a certain task. Focus on doing the task CORRECTLY. e.g. if you want to write an article, don’t just focus on creating the article. Take time to review what you write. Ask yourself “Is this the best thing I should be doing right now?”. Take short breaks every 50 minutes or so.

  • Don’t just tell yourself what you gotta do, over and over again, and hope to eventually get anything done. Instead, make a plan. Focus on doing the very first task of that plan. This first task should be a small one. This way, it’s easier to get the ball rolling.

But I’ll probably forget about all this in the morning. SEE YA.
